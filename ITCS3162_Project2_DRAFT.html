Introduction¶

Whether we like using credit or not, it has been a way for us to "buy now and pay later". The use of credit began as early as the nineteenth century mainly for purchasing homes. In 1899 the first credit bureau named the Atlanta based Retail Credit Company which Recorded credit and other information on Americans. By 1938 the Federal National Mortgage Association currently called Fannie Mae was created as a network for mortgage borrowers, lenders, and investors to connect.

The ability to determine an individual's or business's creditworthy is important. Without this ability, a business could operate at a loss and eventully file for bankruptcy.

The dataset, Default of Credit Card Clients is available from UC Irvine Machine Learning Repository. It is based on research paper written by Che-hui Lien Yeh in 2009 which examined default patterns of Taiwanese customers. The research compared the predictive accuracy of six data mining techniques. For their initial research, neutral network more accurately estimated the real probability of default.

For project two, we will be using classification to predict if an individual will default on their next months payment. Our goal is to evaluate different calssification algorithms to determine the best results for this dataset.

DataSet¶

The dataset is only available in Excel (xls) format and contains characteristics which could help us to determine if a person is creditworthy or not. It contains 30,000 observations and 23 features.

These features are ID, LIMIT_BAL, SEX, EDUCATION, MARRIAGE, AGE, PAY_0, PAY_2, PAY_3, PAY_4, PAY_5, PAY_6, BILL_AMT1, BILL_AMT2, BILL_AMT3, BILL_AMT4, BILL_AMT5, BILL_AMT6, PAY_AMT1, PAY_AMT2, PAY_AMT3, PAY_AMT4, PAY_AMT5, PAY_AMT6, and default payment next month.

Below are descriptions of features based on the information provided from the website:

  • ID is the unique identifier given to each observation
  • LIMIT_BAL is the amount of income for the individual and their family.
  • SEX is the gender of the individual (1 - male, 2 - female)
  • EDUCATION is level of education (1 - graduate school, 2 - university, 3 - high school, 4 - other)
  • MARRIAGE is martial status (1 - married, 2 - single, 3 - other)

  • AGE is age in years.

    Monthly past payment history from April to September, 2005. It was measured on a repayment scale (-1 - payment on time, 1 - one month late, 2 - two months late, 3 - three months late,..., 8 - eight months late, and 9 - 9 months or longer late)

  • PAY_0 is payments missed in September, 2005
  • PAY_2 is payments missed in August, 2005
  • PAY_3 is payments missed in July, 2005
  • PAY_4 is payments missed in June, 2005
  • PAY_5 is payments missed in May, 2005

  • PAY_6 is payments missed in April, 2005

    Billing statement balance

  • BILL_AMT1 is statement balance in September, 2005
  • BILL_AMT2 is statement balance in August, 2005
  • BILL_AMT3 is statement balance in July, 2005
  • BILL_AMT4 is statement balance in June, 2005
  • BILL_AMT5 is statement balance in May, 2005

  • BILL_AMT6 is statement balance in April, 2005

    Previous payment amount

  • PAY_AMT1 is amount paid in September, 2005
  • PAY_AMT2 is amount paid in August, 2005
  • PAY_AMT3 is amount paid in July, 2005
  • PAY_AMT4 is amount paid in June, 2005
  • PAY_AMT5 is amount paid in May, 2005

  • PAY_AMT6 is amount paid in April, 2005

  • default payment next month is our target or response variable. It indicated if an individual defaulted on the next month's payment or not. (1 - Yes, 0 - No)
